## 2. Residency & Key Sovereignty

2.1 **Physical Location.** The Enclave shall be located entirely within the Customer's sovereign territory.

2.2 **Key Control.** Sovereign Keys shall be generated, stored, and used exclusively within hardware security modules (HSMs) physically located in the Customer's territory and accessible only by the Customer's authorized representatives. The Provider shall have no access to Sovereign Keys.

2.2.1 **Post-Quantum Cryptography.** Sovereign Keys shall be generated using NIST-approved post-quantum algorithms: **ML-KEM** (FIPS 203, formerly CRYSTALS-Kyber) for key encapsulation, and **ML-DSA** (FIPS 204, formerly CRYSTALS-Dilithium) for digital signatures — as finalized by NIST on August 13, 2024. Cultural Invariants (such as Ren, Wu Wei, and Dharma) shall be formally verified against quantum-computational threat models to ensure these invariants remain immutable and enforceable in a post-quantum decryption era. Implementations shall target ML-KEM-768 (128-bit security) minimum for key exchange and ML-DSA-65 for signature operations, with upgrade paths to ML-KEM-1024 as threat models evolve.

2.3 **Cultural Invariants.** The Enclave shall enforce the Customer's Cultural Invariants (e.g., Ren and Wu Wei for DragonSeek) as part of the Asha Linter's hardware-rooted formal verification. These invariants are loaded at boot time, immutable during runtime, and cannot be altered by the Provider.

2.4 **Boot Attestation.** The Enclave shall perform hardware-rooted boot attestation (Asha Linter spec) at every startup. The attestation report shall be recorded on the Optical Ledger and made available to the Customer for independent verification.

## 5. Exit & Kill Switch

5.1 **Customer-Initiated Exit.** The Customer may terminate this Addendum at any time. Upon termination, the Provider shall securely erase all Sovereign Data **within 72 hours** in accordance with NIST SP 800-88 (or equivalent), consistent with Constitutional Invariant INV-17 (Right to Delete). The 72-hour window begins at the moment of termination notice receipt and applies to all copies, replicas, backups, and cached fragments across all Provider infrastructure. A cryptographic attestation of erasure — including timestamp, method, and scope — shall be provided and recorded on the Optical Ledger within the same 72-hour window. Failure to complete erasure within this window constitutes a material breach.

5.2 **Kill Switch.** The Enclave shall include a hardware-enforced kill switch that, when triggered by the Customer, shall:
   - Revoke all Sovereign Keys,
   - Immediately erase all volatile memory,
   - Isolate the Enclave from external networks,
   - Migrate the last known good state to a Customer-controlled backup facility within 24 hours.

5.3 **Provider-Initiated Kill Switch.** The Provider may not unilaterally trigger the kill switch except in response to a demonstrable security threat to the Enclave (e.g., physical intrusion). Such a trigger shall be logged, and the Customer shall be notified immediately.

9.1 **Arbitration.** Any dispute arising under this Addendum shall be submitted to the **Court of Last Resort** (multi-civilizational arbitration panel) for binding arbitration. The arbitration shall be conducted in a neutral venue mutually agreed by the parties.

9.2 **Jurisdictional Detection Methodology.** Per Constitutional Invariants INV-33 (Jurisdiction Detection) and INV-34 (Union-Set Jurisdiction), the Enclave shall implement automated jurisdictional detection using a union-set approach with a minimum of **four independent signals**:
   - (a) **Physical location** of data storage and processing hardware (GPS/geofence attestation);
   - (b) **Network origin** of data subjects (IP geolocation, validated against regional Internet registries);
   - (c) **Legal domicile** of the contracting Customer entity (corporate registration jurisdiction);
   - (d) **Citizenship/residency** of data subjects where ascertainable (passport country, declared residency).
   Where signals conflict, the **union-set rule** applies: all implicated jurisdictions' requirements must be satisfied simultaneously. Jurisdictional determinations shall be logged on the Optical Ledger and re-evaluated at minimum every 90 days or upon any material change in Enclave operations.
